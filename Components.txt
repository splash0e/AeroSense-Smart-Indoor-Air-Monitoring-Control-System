1. Arduino UNO
Acts as the main control unit, processing sensor data and executing the automation logic.

2. DHT11 Sensor
Measures temperature and humidity of the surrounding environment.

3. Air Quality Sensor (MQ Series / Equivalent)
Detects gas concentration levels to estimate indoor air quality.

4. 16x2 LCD Display
Displays real-time temperature, humidity, and air quality readings.

5. Relay Module
Works as a switch to control the motor based on signals from the Arduino.

6. DC Motor / Fan (Filter System)
Acts as the air filtration or ventilation mechanism when triggered.

7. Power Supply or Batteries (Solar source)
Provides the required power to run the system components.

8. Jumper Wires & Breadboard
Used for circuit connections and prototyping.

NOTE - 
Future Improvement
Bluetooth Module (HC-05/HC-06)
Can be added to enable wireless monitoring and control via a smartphone.
